Overview
What we're migrating and how SAP fits in

PoP (Point of Purchase / Proof of Performance) is the Distributor claim and credit settlement process. Currently running on a homegrown system built by Johnny. The front end is migrating to Salesforce — SAP remains the back end for billing validation and credit memo creation.

Claim submission flow — three steps
1. Distributor enters claim
Customer material # · quantity · invoice date · account auto-populated
→
2. Salesforce calls SAP
VBRK / VBRP · verifies product + quantities within 24-month window
→
3. Error or credit memo
Fails → Distributor notified · Passes → CR doc created in SAP
Community user / Distributor scoping: The logged-in Community user is linked to a Contact, which is linked to their SAP Account (Distributor). Salesforce uses this to auto-populate the Distributor ID on all claims and search results. The Distributor can only ever see their own data — sharing rules enforce this. Distributor ID is never a visible editable field. ? Contact provisioning TBD — confirm with Venkat how Community users are linked to Distributor contacts
Venkat confirmed: Salesforce does not need real-time pricing pulls. List Price, Distributor Price, and ZCMIR records are all pushed nightly from SAP (background batch jobs). The claim submission flow is straightforward — Distributor enters claim, Salesforce calls SAP to validate, claim either errors out or a credit memo is created.

Real-time interfaces
Claim validation + credit memo creation
REAL-TIMETBDInterface 4 — Billing data validation (VBRK / VBRP) SFDC-25060
At claim submission the Distributor enters their Customer Material # and quantity — their account is auto-populated. Salesforce uses these fields to call SAP billing tables and verify the product and quantities. The query uses the Distributor's Invoice Date as the center of a ±24-month rolling window. Rodel flagged that datasets over 5–10 MB may not be suitable for real-time API — approach TBD at follow-up meeting.

REAL-TIME PUSHInterface 5 — Credit memo creation SFDC-25891
Once a claim passes validation, Salesforce triggers the creation of a Credit Memo (CR document) in SAP. The PoP # generated in Salesforce is mapped to the SAP Purchase Order field on the credit memo. SAP then carries this to the billing document automatically — Salesforce does not write to the billing doc separately.
AttributeDetail
DirectionSalesforce → MuleSoft → SAP (create)
